What is Henna?

Henna is a natural dye derived from the leaves of the henna plant . It's commonly used for temporary body art, particularly in cultures across South Asia, the Middle East, and Africa, often for weddings, celebrations, or cultural traditions.

What's Chemical Henna?

What's Chemical Henna?

Chemical henna, commonly known as "black henna," is a synthetic product that typically contains harmful additives.
Unlike natural henna, it produces a darker color quickly but carries significant risks like:

    - allergic reactions
    - skin irritations
    - long-term health complications
    - scarring

Always double-check where your henna is sourced from.
